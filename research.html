Research

Research in the Data Analytics Group is focused on the discovery and application of new methodologies and technologies for analytics and big data. Please visit the respective section to know more about the research area, publication section and projects section for information regarding latest research papers and specific projects ongoing with collaborations in this laboratory.

Sensors and IoT Networks

Mobile devices like smartphones, tablets, or smartwatches are a gateway to the Internet of Things. They help us to seamlessly communicate with various other devices and systems, such as home automation, entertainment systems, or car systems. The Data Analytics Research Group has profound experience with measurement and analysis of sensor data like pressure, temperature and humidity, acceleration and positioning, heart rate and heart rate variability. We have extensively worked and published quality work addressing various problems such as, data gathering, coverage, lifetime, load distribution, event detection, in-network inference, clustering, routing and many more.

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

Machine learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) may arguably considered as the two of the most revolutionary technologies of this century. The application of ML and AI has been significant in different research domains and in industrial applications as well. The driving idea behind both the technologies being the idea of modelling intelligent behaviour in machines. The applications of AI and ML, as such, have been revolutionary in the context of Data analytics as well. The past decade has witnessed the problem of data changing from collecting data to understanding it, making inferences and strategic decisions.

Automation Systems

Automation systems have played a vital role in conceptualising the ideas of smart city, smart homes, smart transport and many more. The ease of use and automated processes not only enhance the user experience but also are pivotal in streamlining the day to day working of the organizations.
